## Slide 4: Medication Non-Adherence

**Background**: Warm amber + clinical blue

### 💊 **THE HIDDEN DANGER: MISSED MEDICATIONS**

**The Reality**:
- **Polypharmacy** (5+ medications) is extremely common among Dutch elders
- WHO benchmark: **~50% adherence** for chronic therapies in developed countries
- Dutch studies show **35.7% non-adherence** in older adults with polypharmacy (2025 research)
- Missed medications lead to hospitalizations, worsening conditions, and family stress

**Why it happens**:
- Cognitive load of complex schedules
- No one to remind or check
- Fear of side effects not communicated
- No real-time visibility for family or carers

**Citation**: PMC studies on therapeutic adherence in older Dutch adults (2025)

## Slide 6: Fragmented Care Documentation

**Background**: Fragmented puzzle pieces visual

### 📋 **NO SINGLE SOURCE OF TRUTH FOR CARE WORKERS**

**The Current Reality**:
- Multiple carers (thuiszorg, wijkverpleegkundige, huisarts, specialist) work with the same elder
- **No unified, real-time shared record**
- Handover notes are paper-based, WhatsApp messages, or lost in different systems
- Carers often work blind to previous visits, mood changes, or incidents
- This leads to **medication errors, missed safeguarding signals, and poor continuity of care**

**Evidence**:
- Dutch research repeatedly highlights "fragmentation of care" as a major barrier in elderly care
- Buurtzorg and other models have tried to solve this internally, but no national unified solution exists for mixed professional + family care

### 4. **Falls (KOMPAS + ANKER)**

### 5. **Fragmented Care (WACHT)**

- Shared view for all authorised carers + family
